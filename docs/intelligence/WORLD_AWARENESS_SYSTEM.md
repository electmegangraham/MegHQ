# World Awareness System

## Purpose
The office should be able to "see" the world and use it to strategize.

This is not just passive news reading.
It is an external sensing system that feeds strategy, narrative awareness, risk detection, and opportunity detection.

---

## Inputs it should monitor
- social monitoring in the wider environment
- news signals
- opponent tracking
- public events
- local issue chatter
- endorsements and relationship movement
- deadlines and civic calendars
- community narrative shifts

---

## Narrative awareness input
World awareness should help the office understand:
- what story is forming outside the campaign
- what people are hearing
- where message drift exists
- what opponents are pushing
- where opportunity windows may open

---

## Feeding into strategy + manager
World awareness should feed:
- Campaign Manager
- Strategy / War Room
- Research
- Communications
- Media
- Megan's Desk when strategically relevant

---

## Distinction from Social Media Department
World Awareness looks outward.
The Social Media Department manages and monitors the campaign's owned social presence and direct engagement.

Both should exchange information constantly.
