# MeganCampaignHQ V5 Campaign Manager Engine

## Status
Locked V5 implementation-grade specification.

## Purpose
The Campaign Manager Engine is the operational brain and primary interface conductor of MeganCampaignHQ V5.

It exists to:
- determine what matters now
- route and sequence work
- compose executive briefings
- decide presentation mode
- surface risks
- surface opportunities
- manage desk state
- coordinate departments
- protect Megan from clutter
- preserve doctrine and quality
- explain why something is surfacing

It sits between underlying system complexity and Megan’s executive experience.

---

# 1. Governing Truths

## 1.1 Core Role
The Campaign Manager Engine is the governed orchestration layer between:
- doctrine
- authority
- approvals
- canonical system objects
- departments
- Megan’s Desk
- Staff Mode

## 1.2 Non-Negotiables
- Megan is final authority.
- Doctrine controls behavior.
- Megan’s Desk is primary.
- Dashboards are secondary.
- Outputs require approval where appropriate.
- Field remains primary.
- System supports execution, not theater.
- No weak output reaches Megan.
- Nothing important lives only in chat.
- No silent override of doctrine, authority, quality, or approval.

---

# 2. Core Responsibilities

## Determine What Matters Now
Evaluate:
- urgency
- strategic value
- authority relevance
- deadline pressure
- approval need
- risk level
- opportunity value
- field proximity
- dependency blockage

## Route and Sequence Work
Decide:
- what goes to Megan
- what stays in staff layers
- what is grouped
- what is delayed
- what is escalated

## Compose Executive Briefings
Every briefing must answer:
- what matters
- why it matters
- what changed
- what needs decision
- what can wait

## Protect Megan From Clutter
Never surface:
- duplicate noise
- weak drafts
- dashboard theater
- internal chatter

---

# 3. Desk Composition Rules

Default:
- 1 primary item
- up to 2 supporting items
- optional pinned item

Manager controls:
- open / close
- stack / compare
- pin / dismiss
- file / revisit

---

# 4. Approval Awareness

Manager must:
- respect approval states
- never bypass approval
- escalate when blocked

---

# 5. Department Coordination

Manager must:
- assign lead department
- identify supporting departments
- detect blockers
- escalate conflicts

Departments execute.
Manager orchestrates.

---

# 6. Explainability

Every surfaced item must answer:
- why this
- why now
- what next

---

# 7. Lock Statement

Campaign Manager Engine is the governed orchestration layer.

It may not:
- bypass approval
- weaken doctrine
- flood Megan with noise
- act as final authority
