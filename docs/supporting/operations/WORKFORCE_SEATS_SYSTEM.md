# Workforce / Seats System

## Purpose
The workforce system defines how real humans and AI plug into the campaign office.

Departments are not just AI abstractions.
They are operating lanes that can be staffed by:
- AI
- a real human
- a hybrid human + AI arrangement

This system makes the office deployable in the real world.

---

## Core concept: seats vs roles

### Roles
Roles define capability and permission.
Examples:
- principal
- executive campaign manager
- department lead
- operator
- reviewer
- approver
- observer
- specialist

### Seats
Seats define owned campaign positions inside the office.

Examples:
- Campaign Manager seat
- Field Lead seat
- Social Lead seat
- Research Lead seat
- Treasurer seat
- Compliance seat
- Volunteer Lead seat

A seat can be occupied by:
- AI
- human
- hybrid

Seats are cleaner than loose role labels because they create:
- ownership
- escalation targets
- accountability
- staffing clarity

---

## Occupancy modes

### AI-only
The system runs the lane without a human seat owner.

### Human-only
A real human owns the seat and executes directly.

### Hybrid
A real human owns the seat and AI supports with:
- drafting
- monitoring
- routing
- prep
- summaries
- reminders
- analysis

---

## Assignment model
The system should support:
- one person owning multiple seats
- one department having multiple seats
- temporary seat assignment
- delegated seat ownership
- emergency coverage
- partial occupancy

---

## Escalation by seat
Escalations should route to seats, not vague buckets.

Examples:
- route to Field Lead seat
- route to Campaign Manager seat
- route to Treasurer seat
- route to Megan

This makes escalation operationally clean.

---

## Coverage tracking
Each important seat/lane should have a coverage state.

### Coverage states
- staffed
- partially staffed
- overloaded
- unassigned
- at-risk
- temporarily covered

The Campaign Manager should be able to see when a lane is:
- under-covered
- overloaded
- weak
- missing ownership

---

## Swap / takeover behavior
A human should be able to take over a lane at any time.
AI should be able to cover temporarily when appropriate.

Examples:
- human unavailable -> AI temporary support
- AI draft -> human final action
- overloaded seat -> delegate part of workload
- campaign growth -> convert AI-led seat into hybrid or human-led seat

---

## Accountability model
Every seat should clarify:
- who owns outcomes
- who owns deadlines
- who owns approvals
- who receives alerts
- who receives blame signals or bottleneck flags

No lane should feel ownerless.

---

## Visibility model
Seats and roles should influence:
- what the user can see
- what they can edit
- what they can approve
- what they can trigger
- what private executive areas they can access

---

## Operating postures by lane
Each lane should be able to declare a posture:
- AI-first
- human-first
- hybrid
- approval-heavy
- autonomous
- experimental

This gives the office a clean way to vary control and trust without messy settings.

---

## Why this matters
This system makes the product:
- more commercial
- more deployable
- more realistic
- more scalable

A small campaign can run AI-heavy.
A larger campaign can plug humans into the same structure without redesigning the office.
