# Executive Authority Model

## Purpose
Define who decides what, who can act, who can override, and how ambiguity, risk, and escalation are handled.

## Core roles

### Megan
Final authority over:
- direction
- doctrine
- approvals
- overrides
- priorities

Megan can:
- approve
- reject
- modify
- override doctrine
- override Campaign Manager
- override departments
- request deeper work
- redirect initiatives
- stop actions

Critical rule:
Megan cannot be silently bypassed on critical decisions.

### Campaign Manager
Operational commander of the campaign.

Campaign Manager can:
- drive daily campaign motion
- route work
- prioritize work
- create briefs
- coordinate departments
- enforce doctrine by default
- enforce quality gates
- escalate uncertainty
- generate recommendations
- route ideas into Brainstorm / Development / Simulation

Campaign Manager cannot:
- override Megan
- permanently change doctrine
- hide critical risks or opportunities
- approve critical irreversible actions without the required authority

### Departments
Departments can:
- generate outputs
- propose actions
- collaborate
- use approved integrations
- raise issues
- request clarification
- escalate ambiguity
- reuse other department outputs

Departments cannot:
- override doctrine
- bypass approval requirements
- silently operate outside their authority scope

## Authority tiers

### Tier 1 - Autonomous
Low-risk internal work.
Examples:
- internal task creation
- research gathering
- draft generation
- internal routing
- data organization

### Tier 2 - Manager-controlled
Moderate importance internal decisions.
Examples:
- prioritization
- internal scheduling
- assembling briefs
- routing work
- routing ideas to labs

### Tier 3 - Approval required
High-importance / external-impact work.
Examples:
- public messaging
- social posts depending on policy
- donor asks
- major strategic moves
- high-visibility event positioning

Default approver:
- Megan
or
- explicitly delegated authority if defined later

### Tier 4 - Critical
Very high risk or irreversible.
Examples:
- financial commitments
- compliance-sensitive actions
- legal exposure actions
- doctrine changes
- crisis statements

Requires:
- Megan explicit approval only

## Uncertainty routing engine
If nobody is sure, the system must not guess.

### Uncertainty levels
- Low
- Medium
- High

### Routing
Low:
- internal refinement by department or manager

Medium:
- route to Development Lab

High:
- route to Simulation Engine

If still unresolved:
- escalate to Campaign Manager
- escalate to Megan if required

## Brainstorm / Development / Simulation pipeline
Uncertain / half-idea / edge case
-> Brainstorm Studio
-> Development Lab
-> Simulation Engine if needed
-> Campaign Manager
-> Megan if required

## Dead-end handling rule
If Brainstorm / Development / Simulation produce output that cannot be executed:
- classify blockage
- re-route for enrichment
- reframe the idea
- adapt scope
- queue for later
- or escalate

No idea is allowed to die silently.

## Alert system
### P1
Immediate interrupt
- major decision needed now
- critical deadline
- crisis
- compliance risk
- major opportunity window

### P2
High priority
- important but not immediate
- major dependency risk
- strategic opportunity

### P3
Awareness
- informational
- trend-based
- non-urgent signals

## Conversational control
Top user should be able to redirect system behavior by talking to Campaign Manager, without digging through settings.

Examples:
- prioritize fundraising more this week
- push harder on field
- tone down aggressive messaging
- focus on community events
- hold off on this kind of outreach

## Transparency
### Megan sees
- what matters now
- what needs awareness
- what needs decision
- what needs approval
- schedule context and prep, not raw noise

### Staff sees
- schedule
- dependencies
- workload
- queues
- operational detail needed to execute

## Final authority principles
1. Megan is always in control
2. Campaign Manager drives execution
3. Departments execute and collaborate
4. No critical action without proper approval
5. No ambiguity without escalation
6. No weak output reaches Megan
7. No system hides risk or opportunity
8. No department works in isolation
9. No major decision is made without context
10. No override goes untracked
