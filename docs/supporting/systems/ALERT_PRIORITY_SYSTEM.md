# Alert + Priority System

## Purpose
The office must distinguish:
- what matters now
- what matters today
- what matters later
- what is only background awareness

This system governs urgency, interruption, escalation, and attention routing.

---

## Alert tiers

### P1 - Immediate
Examples:
- major decision required now
- critical deadline
- crisis
- compliance risk
- major opportunity window

Behavior:
- may interrupt Megan immediately
- compress context
- present options
- recommend action

### P2 - High priority
Examples:
- major dependency risk
- strong opportunity
- important but not immediate item

Behavior:
- prominently surfaced
- desk now or today
- staff mode elevated

### P3 - Awareness
Examples:
- non-urgent pattern
- informational trend
- low-immediacy signals

Behavior:
- awareness layer
- monitor over time
- avoid unnecessary interruption

---

## Interrupt rules
The system should interrupt Megan only when:
- decision is urgent and important
- consequence of delay is meaningful
- compliance/legal exposure exists
- opportunity window is closing
- schedule disruption requires immediate adjustment

---

## Escalation triggers
Examples:
- overdue critical work
- repeated unresolved ambiguity
- doctrine conflict with immediate consequence
- bottleneck affecting execution
- no owner on critical lane
- campaign narrative drift with visible risk

---

## Decision urgency model
Every major decision should be framed with:
- urgency
- impact
- reversibility
- cost of delay

This helps the Campaign Manager distinguish:
- important but not urgent
- urgent but low impact
- high impact and time-sensitive

---

## Timing intelligence
The system should know not only what matters, but when to surface it for maximum usefulness.
Examples:
- prep before an event, not after
- donor ask timing before opportunity closes
- narrative warning before message drift becomes public
